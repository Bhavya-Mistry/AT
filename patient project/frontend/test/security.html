Encryption

All data is encrypted at every stage of its lifecycle:

At rest: AES-256

All stored health records, files, and messages are encrypted with AES-256-GCM. Encryption keys are managed by a dedicated key management service and rotated annually.

In transit: TLS 1.3

All data between your browser, mobile app, and our servers is encrypted using TLS 1.3. We enforce HSTS and certificate pinning on mobile clients.

Messages: End-to-end

Consultation messages are end-to-end encrypted. MediConnect support staff cannot read your conversations. Only you and your assigned doctor have access.

Passwords: bcrypt

Passwords are hashed using bcrypt with a cost factor of 12. We never store plaintext passwords and salt every hash individually.

Infrastructure

MediConnect runs on a purpose-built, healthcare-grade cloud infrastructure:

  • Multi-region redundancy — data replicated across three geographic regions for resilience
  • Zero-trust networking — every service must authenticate to every other service; no implicit trust
  • Private VPC — all internal services are isolated in a private network with no public internet exposure
  • DDoS protection — enterprise-grade DDoS mitigation on all endpoints
  • Automated backups — encrypted backups every 6 hours, with 30-day retention and point-in-time recovery
  • 99.9% SLA uptime — see our Status Page for live metrics

Access controls

We follow the principle of least privilege throughout:

  • No MediConnect employee can read your health records or messages
  • Doctors only gain access to a patient's records when the patient initiates a consultation with them
  • Admin access to production systems requires hardware MFA and is reviewed quarterly
  • All privileged access is logged and subject to automated anomaly detection
  • Employee access is revoked within 1 hour of termination

You can view a complete log of all access to your account from Account Settings → Security → Access Log.

Security testing

Our security posture is continuously validated:

  • Annual penetration testing by an independent third-party security firm
  • Continuous automated vulnerability scanning on all code, dependencies, and infrastructure
  • Bug bounty programme for responsible disclosure of security vulnerabilities
  • Code review requirements — all code changes require security review before deployment
  • Dependency monitoring — automated alerts for known vulnerabilities in third-party dependencies

Incident response

In the event of a security incident, our response protocol is:

  1. Immediate containment within 1 hour of detection
  2. Assessment of scope and impact within 4 hours
  3. Notification to affected users within 24 hours (faster if required by law)
  4. Full incident report published within 72 hours
  5. Regulatory notification within timeframes required by HIPAA and applicable law

In 4 years of operation, MediConnect has never experienced a breach affecting patient PHI.
