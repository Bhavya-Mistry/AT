Cookie Policy

A clear explanation of how ClinIQ uses cookies — and our commitment to using them minimally.

Effective date: 1 January 2025 Last updated: 1 March 2025

Short version: We use only essential cookies required to run the platform securely. We do not use advertising, tracking, or third-party analytics cookies. Your health data is never tied to cookie data.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They serve various purposes — from keeping you logged in, to tracking your behaviour across the internet for advertising purposes. We take a minimal approach: we only use cookies that are strictly necessary to provide our services.

Cookies we use

Cookie name Type Purpose Duration
mc_session Essential Maintains your authenticated session. Required for login to function. Session
mc_csrf Essential Cross-site request forgery protection token. Required for form security. Session
mc_pref Essential Stores your display preferences (e.g. theme, language) to persist across sessions. 1 year
mc_anon Analytics Anonymous, non-identifiable usage statistics. Used to measure page performance. Contains no personal or health data. Opt-out available. 90 days

Cookies we do NOT use

We have made a deliberate choice not to use the following:

  • Advertising cookies — we do not show ads and do not track users for ad targeting
  • Social media pixels — no Facebook, Instagram, or LinkedIn tracking pixels
  • Third-party analytics — no Google Analytics, Hotjar, or similar tools that send data to third parties
  • Fingerprinting — we do not use device fingerprinting techniques to track you across sessions or sites

Your choices

Essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are required for the platform to function. Attempting to block them will prevent login.

Analytics cookies can be opted out in your account settings under Privacy → Cookie Preferences. You can also set your browser to reject all cookies, but this may affect your ability to use the platform.

Third-party services

ClinIQ uses the following third-party services that may set their own cookies:

  • Google Sign-In — if you use "Sign in with Google", Google may set authentication cookies governed by Google's Privacy Policy
  • Payment processor — if you make a payment, our PCI-DSS certified payment provider may set cookies for fraud prevention. These are isolated to the payment flow and never linked to your health data.

Changes to this policy

If we add new cookies or change our approach, we will update this policy and notify you by email before the change takes effect.

Questions? Contact privacy@cliniq.health or visit our Privacy Policy.
