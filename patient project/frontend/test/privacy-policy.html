Privacy Policy

We believe your health data is yours. This policy explains exactly how we handle it — in plain language, without legal sleight of hand.

Plain-English summary: We collect only what's needed to provide you care. We never sell your data. All health records are encrypted. You can request deletion at any time. Doctors only see your records when you initiate a consultation with them.

1. Information we collect

Account information

When you create an account, we collect your email address and a hashed password (we never store your password in plain text). You may also provide your name, date of birth, and contact details.

Health information

Any information you provide during AI consultations or in your health profile, including symptoms, medical history, medications, allergies, and conditions, is stored as Protected Health Information (PHI) under HIPAA.

Uploaded files

Medical files you upload — lab results, scan images, prescriptions — are stored encrypted in our secure cloud infrastructure. We do not process or analyse these files beyond facilitating your care.

Usage data

We collect standard server logs including IP address, browser type, and pages visited. This data is used for security monitoring and platform improvement only. It is never linked to your health records.

2. How we use your information

Your information is used exclusively to:

  • Provide AI consultation and triage services
  • Connect you with appropriate physicians
  • Maintain your health record and consultation history
  • Send important service communications (appointment reminders, security alerts)
  • Improve our AI model's clinical accuracy through anonymised, aggregated analysis

We do not use your data for advertising, marketing profiling, or sale to third parties.

3. When we share your information

Your health information is shared only in these specific circumstances:

  • With your treating physician — when you initiate a consultation, the assigned doctor can access your health profile and the consultation history
  • With cloud infrastructure providers — encrypted data stored with HIPAA-compliant cloud providers under BAAs (see below)
  • When legally required — we will comply with valid legal process, and will notify you where permitted by law
  • With your explicit consent — if you use our secure-share feature to send records to an external provider

We will never sell, rent, or trade your health information to insurers, employers, pharmaceutical companies, or data brokers — ever.

4. HIPAA and healthcare privacy

ClinIQ is a Covered Entity under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). This means:

  • Your health data is treated as Protected Health Information (PHI)
  • All employees with PHI access complete mandatory HIPAA training
  • We maintain Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all service providers who handle PHI
  • We maintain a Notice of Privacy Practices as required by HIPAA
  • You have the right to an accounting of disclosures of your PHI

Enterprise customers may request a BAA Agreement to use ClinIQ as a Business Associate.

5. Data retention

We retain your health records for a minimum of 7 years from the date of creation, as required by medical record-keeping laws in most jurisdictions. After this period, records are permanently deleted unless you request deletion sooner (subject to legal minimums).

Usage logs are retained for 90 days, after which they are permanently purged.

6. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of all personal data we hold about you
  • Rectification — correct inaccurate personal data
  • Erasure — request deletion of your account and associated data (subject to legal minimums)
  • Portability — receive your health records in a machine-readable format
  • Restriction — limit how we process your data in certain circumstances
  • Objection — object to certain types of processing

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@cliniq.health or use the data request form in your account settings.

7. Security measures

We protect your data with multiple layers of security. See our Security page for the complete technical overview. In summary:

  • AES-256 encryption at rest for all stored data
  • TLS 1.3 encryption for all data in transit
  • Zero-knowledge architecture — our support staff cannot read your messages or health records
  • Full access audit logging visible to you
  • Annual third-party penetration testing
  • SOC 2 Type II certified infrastructure

8. Cookies

We use essential cookies to maintain your session and prevent fraud. We do not use third-party advertising or tracking cookies. See our Cookie Policy for full details.

9. Children's privacy

ClinIQ is not directed at children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has created an account, contact us immediately at privacy@cliniq.health and we will delete the account. For patients aged 13–17, parental consent is required during account creation.

10. Contact us

For privacy-related questions, data requests, or to report a concern:

  • Email: privacy@cliniq.health
  • Post: ClinIQ Privacy Office, 100 Health Plaza, San Francisco, CA 94102
  • Response time: We will respond to all privacy requests within 30 days

If you are unsatisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant data protection authority in your jurisdiction.
