Business Associate Agreement

For healthcare organisations using ClinIQ in a HIPAA-covered capacity. Our BAA ensures your use of the platform meets federal privacy law requirements.

What is a BAA? Under HIPAA, if your organisation is a Covered Entity (such as a hospital, clinic, or health plan) and you use a third-party service that handles Protected Health Information on your behalf, you are required to have a Business Associate Agreement in place. ClinIQ serves as a Business Associate in this relationship.

Who needs a BAA?

You need a BAA with ClinIQ if your organisation is:

  • A healthcare provider (hospital, clinic, medical practice, pharmacy)
  • A health plan (insurance company, HMO, Medicare/Medicaid)
  • A healthcare clearinghouse
  • A Business Associate of a Covered Entity that processes PHI through our platform

Individual patients using ClinIQ for personal healthcare do not require a BAA — this is handled within our standard Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.

What our BAA covers

PHI safeguarding

We agree to implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect PHI as required by the HIPAA Security Rule.

Permitted uses

PHI is used only to provide healthcare operations services on your behalf. No secondary uses without explicit permission.

Breach notification

We will notify you within 60 days of discovering a breach of unsecured PHI, as required by the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule.

Individual rights

We support your obligations to provide individuals access to, and accounting of, their PHI in a timely manner.

Subcontractor BAAs

We ensure all subcontractors who may access PHI have appropriate BAAs in place with ClinIQ.

Termination & return

Upon termination of the BAA, we will return or destroy all PHI as directed and certify that all PHI has been secured.

Standard BAA terms

Our standard BAA is based on the HHS model Business Associate Agreement and includes all required provisions under 45 CFR §164.308, §164.310, §164.312, and §164.316. Key terms include:

  • Term: Coterminous with the underlying service agreement
  • Governing law: Federal law (HIPAA/HITECH) and the laws of the State of California
  • Liability allocation: Each party is responsible for its own HIPAA compliance obligations
  • Audit rights: We will cooperate with HHS OCR audits and investigations
  • Amendment: We may amend the BAA to comply with changes in HIPAA regulations with 30 days notice

Requesting a BAA

Enterprise and institutional customers can request our standard BAA through the following process:

  1. Contact our enterprise team at enterprise@cliniq.health
  2. Provide your organisation name, type (Covered Entity or Business Associate), and primary contact
  3. Our legal team will send the BAA via DocuSign within 2 business days
  4. Once countersigned, the BAA is effective immediately

If you require custom BAA terms, our legal team is available to negotiate amendments. Note that material deviations from our standard form may require additional review time.
