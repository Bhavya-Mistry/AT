Business Associate Agreement

This Agreement governs ClinIQ's handling of Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of Covered Entities under HIPAA. By creating an account and checking the BAA box, you acknowledge and agree to these terms.

Effective date: 1 January 2025 Last updated: 1 March 2025

Plain-English summary: This BAA makes ClinIQ responsible for protecting any health information it handles on your behalf. We will not use or share your PHI for any purpose other than providing our services. You retain ownership of all patient data at all times.

1. Definitions

The following terms have the meanings given under HIPAA (45 CFR Parts 160 and 164) and the HITECH Act:

  • Covered Entity — a healthcare provider, health plan, or healthcare clearinghouse subject to HIPAA.
  • Business Associate (ClinIQ) — a person or entity that performs functions or activities on behalf of a Covered Entity involving the use or disclosure of PHI.
  • Protected Health Information (PHI) — individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium.
  • Breach — an impermissible acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI that compromises the security or privacy of such information.
  • Security Incident — the attempted or successful unauthorised access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations.

2. ClinIQ's obligations

As your Business Associate, ClinIQ agrees to:

  • Not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by this Agreement or as required by law.
  • Use appropriate safeguards and comply with the HIPAA Security Rule with respect to electronic PHI.
  • Report to you any use or disclosure of PHI not provided for by this Agreement of which ClinIQ becomes aware, including any security incidents.
  • Ensure that any subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of ClinIQ agree to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to ClinIQ under this Agreement.
  • Make PHI available to you as necessary to fulfil your obligations to provide individuals with access to their PHI.
  • Make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services for the purpose of determining compliance with HIPAA.
  • Return or destroy all PHI received from you, or created or received by ClinIQ on your behalf, upon termination of this Agreement, where feasible.

3. Permitted uses of PHI

ClinIQ may use or disclose PHI only in the following circumstances:

  • To provide the services described in the ClinIQ Terms of Service on your behalf.
  • For the proper management and administration of ClinIQ, provided that disclosures are required by law or ClinIQ obtains reasonable assurances that the PHI will be held confidentially.
  • To carry out the legal responsibilities of ClinIQ, under the same conditions described above.
  • To provide data aggregation services relating to your healthcare operations, as permitted by HIPAA.
  • As required by law, including in response to a valid court order, subpoena, or government authority.

ClinIQ will not use or disclose PHI for any marketing or commercial purposes, and will never sell PHI.

4. Safeguards

ClinIQ implements and maintains industry-standard technical, physical, and administrative safeguards to protect PHI, including:

  • AES-256 encryption for all PHI at rest.
  • TLS 1.3 encryption for all PHI in transit.
  • Role-based access controls limiting PHI access to authorised personnel only.
  • Full audit logging of all access to PHI, available to you on request.
  • Annual third-party security assessments and penetration testing.
  • Employee training on HIPAA compliance and PHI handling.

ClinIQ will maintain a comprehensive information security programme consistent with the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C).

5. Breach notification

ClinIQ will notify you without unreasonable delay, and in no case later than 60 calendar days after discovery, of any Breach of unsecured PHI. Such notification will include, to the extent possible:

  • A description of the Breach, including the date of the Breach and date of discovery.
  • A description of the types of PHI involved.
  • Any steps individuals should take to protect themselves from potential harm.
  • A description of what ClinIQ is doing to investigate the Breach, mitigate harm, and protect against further Breaches.
  • Contact information for you to ask questions or learn additional information.

ClinIQ will also report to you any Security Incidents of which it becomes aware, even if the incident did not result in a Breach.

6. Individual rights

ClinIQ will support your obligations to individuals under HIPAA by:

  • Making PHI available to individuals who request access to their records within the timeframes required by HIPAA.
  • Amending PHI in a designated record set at your direction and in accordance with HIPAA.
  • Providing an accounting of disclosures of PHI to individuals upon your request.
  • Incorporating any restrictions agreed to by you pursuant to an individual's request that ClinIQ restrict the use or disclosure of their PHI.

7. Subcontractors

ClinIQ will ensure that any subcontractors or agents to whom it delegates functions, activities, or services that involve the creation, receipt, maintenance, or transmission of PHI execute a written agreement with ClinIQ that contains the same restrictions, conditions, and requirements applicable to ClinIQ under this Agreement. ClinIQ remains liable for the acts and omissions of its subcontractors to the same extent as its own acts and omissions.

8. Term and termination

This Agreement is effective from the date you create your ClinIQ account and remains in effect until the termination of the underlying service relationship between you and ClinIQ.

Either party may terminate this Agreement if the other party commits a material breach of any provision and fails to cure such breach within 30 days of written notice. Upon termination, ClinIQ will, at your election, return or securely destroy all PHI held on your behalf and provide written certification of such destruction. Where return or destruction is not feasible, the protections of this Agreement will continue to apply to any retained PHI.

9. Governing law

This Agreement is governed by federal law, including HIPAA and the HITECH Act, and the laws of the State of California. Any dispute arising under or related to this Agreement will be resolved in the courts of Santa Clara County, California, and both parties consent to personal jurisdiction and venue in such courts.

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, and understandings. ClinIQ may amend this Agreement to comply with changes in HIPAA regulations, with 30 days written notice to you.

10. Acknowledgement

By checking the BAA acknowledgement box during account registration, you confirm that:

  • You have read and understood this Business Associate Agreement in its entirety.
  • You have the authority to enter into this Agreement on behalf of your organisation (if applicable).
  • You agree to be bound by the terms of this Agreement.
  • Individual patients using ClinIQ for personal health management acknowledge this Agreement as part of the standard service terms and do not require a separate enterprise BAA.

Enterprise organisations requiring a countersigned BAA for compliance purposes should contact enterprise@cliniq.health. Our legal team will provide a formal, executed copy within 2 business days.
